# ðŸŽ® Video Game Sales Analysis
This project aims to provide insightful data on the video game industry for game publishers. Using Kaggle's [Video Game Sales](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/gregorut/videogamesales) data from 1971 to 2016 and [IGDB API](https://api-docs.igdb.com/#getting-started), I pulled data including but not limited to regional sales, genre & publishers, and engagement scores. In this project, I present business metrics as well as correlations between popularity score (provided by IGDB) to sales. 

## ðŸ’¡ Purpose
Publishers get useful insights into how their games perform under various factors and help them make decisions for future marketing and release of their games. I present these findings using a Power BI dashboard.

## ðŸ“š Data
The dataset combines historical video game sales data from [Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/gregorut/videogamesales) with popularity and metadata from the [IGDB API](https://api-docs.igdb.com/#getting-started), providing a consolidated view of the global gaming market over time.

## ðŸ’Ž Insights
* Gaming industry rewards hits, but not as much as other media like in [film](https://deadline.com/2024/01/international-box-office-2023-global-studio-rankings-market-share-1235709538/).
  * Top 10% capture 36% of sales 
  * Mid-tier games ($1.7M-$5.0M in sales) remain viable
  * Implication: More room for AA studios and indie breakouts
* Global gaming market shows mature equilibrium across genres.
  * Shooters over-index in NA (58% of sales) vs Japan (2.9%), while RPG sales in Japan contribute to 32% of the global sales
* Nintendo platforms dominate: Wii (4.3M/title), GB (3.5M), NES (3.4M) lead the industry.
  * XBOX 360's 212 titles average only 2.9M vs Wii's 140 at 4.3M
  * High quality is favorable over maximizing releases
* NA dominates gaming industry by 50% of global sales, with 29% Europe, 12% Japan, and 9% for other regions.
